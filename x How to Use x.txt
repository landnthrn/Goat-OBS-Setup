HOW TO SETUP 

~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

STEP 1: HOW TO IMPORT SETTING PROFILES:

— Click the Profile tab at the top of OBS  

— Select Import  

— Import the profile folder one by one

~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

STEP 2. HOW TO IMPORT SCENE COLLECTIONS:

— Click the Scene Collection tab at the top of OBS  

— Select Import  

— Import the .json file(s) you want

You will see missing media warnings, dismiss for now, the `ADJUST FOR YOUR SETUP` section will go over this.

~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

STEP 3. HOW TO IMPORT THEMES:

— Download theme .zip(s) from the official site:
  https://obsproject.com/forum/resources/categories/themes.10/

— Place .zip(s) inside `C:\Program Files\obs-studio\data\obs-studio\themes` 

— Extract .zip(s)

— Restart OBS

— In OBS Settings > Appearance > Theme, you should see your imported themes

~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

STEP 4. PLUGINS / ADD-ONS / TOOLS:

These are some handy add-on suggestions for OBS,

— WebCam Backdrops Collection:
  https://drive.google.com/file/d/1xXz3OhqebLkwBnuE3A5Hb1OiN8m0CoOK/view?usp=sharing

— Casterlabs:
  https://casterlabs.co
  Streaming - Chat, widgets, alerts, & more

  Once installed, you can use my personal setup from the `My Casterlabs Setup` folder in this repo.
  Just copy & replace the files inside that folder into `C:\Users\%USERNAME%\AppData\Roaming\casterlabs-caffeinated\preferences`

— Handbrake:
  https://handbrake.fr
  Compress/downsize video files, seperate application

— Streamfog:
  https://streamfog.com
  The impressive alternative to the old Snapcamera, face/camera filters & real-time AR effects.
  Use the `Chroma Key` filter on the `STREAMFOG CAM` source to adjust for background mattes coming from Streamfog.

— Live AI Face Swap:
  https://github.com/hacksider/Deep-Live-Cam
  Runs a local AI model on your computer to generate accurate faceswaps with any face to yours
  They offer a free version which is unlimited, but a techy setup not easy for beginners. 

— Zoom to Mouse.lua:
  https://github.com/BlankSourceCode/obs-zoom-to-mouse
  https://obsproject.com/forum/resources/zoom-to-mouse.1823/
  Camera zooms and smoothly follows/live tracks your mouse around a screen

— NVIDIA Broadcast Download Resources: (For NVIDIA Users)
  https://www.nvidia.com/en-us/geforce/broadcasting/broadcast-sdk/resources/
  Likely the most effective options for effects/filters like background removal, and audio enhancement.
  Download the installer for Audio Effects & Video Effects that match your NVIDIA GPU Series. 
  Once you've run the installers & restart OBS, the filter will show in the filter properties of a source in OBS.

If you don't end up getting Streamfog or the AI Face Swap additions, just remove the scenes created for them. 

~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

STEP 5. ADJUST FOR YOUR SETUP:

— If you haven't already, install the WebCam Backdrops collection here:
  https://drive.google.com/file/d/1xXz3OhqebLkwBnuE3A5Hb1OiN8m0CoOK/view?usp=sharing

   Right click the sources in the `BACKGROUNDS` scene, select Properties > Browse and point the missing media files to the location you have the WebCam Backdrop videos downloaded 
  (wherever you always plan to keep them, otherwise you'll need to repoint again)
  
— In OBS Settings > Output tab, 

  - Adjust streaming bitrate according to the platform(s) you stream on:
    Twitch = 6000-8000 Kpbs
    Youtube = 10000 Kpbs
    Kick = 8000-10000 Kpbs
    Most others = 8000-10000 Kpbs

  - If your GPU is not NVIDIA change Video Encoder to x264. If you see more options, research what's best for your GPU.
    Make sure to do this for recording tab as well.

  - Repeat this for all profiles to keep them synced!

— In OBS Settings > Video tab,

  - Adjust canvas size to your monitor resolution.
    If the canvas resolution is higher than what you want to output, downscale using Lanczos. 

  - Also in Video tab, adjust the frame rate to your liking

  - Repeat this for all profiles to keep them synced!

— Right click `MIC` source > Filters, 

  - Adjust the filters to what sounds nice for your audio hardware/mic. 
    (The compressor filter default settings are usually great without needing adjustment.)

   You can hear yourself for testing by selecting the gear icon bottom left of the Audio Mixer dock, 
   for the `MIC` source listed change the `Audio Monitoring` option to `Monitor Only` or `Monitor and Output`, switch back later.

  - Do the same with `AUDIO` source, adjust filters to your liking
